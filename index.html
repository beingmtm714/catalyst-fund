01 · Program architecture

Build the infrastructurePlug in the expertise

The lasting asset is the infrastructure, not the subject: the mechanics, rubric, and compliance that keep federal money flowing. Build it once; it runs for any vertical.

The health expertise plugs into that core through the advisory board. Top healthtech VCs from across the country join for what the fund returns: dealflow, a co-investor to help fill out their rounds, and pilot access to Louisiana's anchor systems for their portfolio. They source and vet per problem area, engaged for that exchange rather than carried on payroll.

The same plug-in scales. It runs every vertical LA.IO names next, and it stands up a venture studio, where EIRs ideate alongside us and the founders often come through those same VCs.

02 · Needs assessment

Start from real demand

Run it like founder discovery, not a health survey. Start with the people who know the market and those who live the need.

1
Advisory board

Top health-tech VCs, Louisiana systems, ecosystem players.

2
Discovery

Interview need, from mutual-aid care to the largest systems.

3
Problem areas

Rank and name the priorities, scored on clear criteria.

4
Set the narrative

Frame the investment around need, not an open window.

What a problem area looks like
Maternal health Behavioral health Rural care delivery Chronic disease management Diagnostics access + whatever discovery surfaces
Each area scored
People reached × Market size × Upside → weighted by fit to the outcomes
The success rubric is an output, not an input

A gain in maternal health isn't measured like behavioral health. The board and providers define the metrics per problem area, and that becomes the bar every funded company meets.

How each $20M tranche aims
Model A · Several themes

A few priority areas per round

Publish three or four areas for the tranche (say maternal, behavioral, and rural delivery). Companies across all of them have context, and the fund stays flexible. The trade: sourcing energy splits, and a weak area can still draw the round's capital.

Model B · One area · favored

The whole $20M at one problem

Point the full tranche at a single area (maternal health for 2027). Founders self-select, sourcing drives hard against one target, and the state's pull concentrates. The trade: it's a bigger bet on one area being right.

03 · Active sourcing

Source the marketDon't wait for it

The state's best offer isn't the check. It's the customer. Capital is easy for a strong company to find. A warm path into the institutions that touch most of Louisiana's patients, Ochsner, FMOL, and Louisiana Blue, is not.

Inbound only

Adverse selection

An open window selects for founders who couldn't raise elsewhere.

Outbound

Source against the need

Go after the named problem areas through people who know the market.

Local first · the priority

Surface Louisiana founders who can win

Operators here who could raise from Silicon Valley or NYC VCs. The bench is thin, so the bar stays real: back the genuine shots and get them to a round an outside VC leads.

National · fill the bench

Recruit proven companies to Louisiana

The pitch: the fund fills out the round on an uncapped SAFE, opens pilots with Louisiana's anchor providers and payer, and makes the state a near-term expansion market.

Why a national founder says yes to Louisiana
Two logos, most of the state · Ochsner & FMOL anchor acute care A digital-forward anchor · Ochsner runs remote-care at scale, wired to adopt and measure new tools Worst-ranked health in the US · measurable impact to point to ~2M on Medicaid · value-based and rural-care fit One dominant insurer · Louisiana Blue can underwrite pilots
Open question · value back to rural Louisiana

When it fits, a side letter ties the SAFE to local hiring, a customer pipeline, and revenue that touches the tax base. Hiring is the hard ask. Worth working through together.

04 · How the money works

Capital that helps, on terms founders accept

Cohort 1 awards are milestone-based grants today. Milestones keep the state accountable, but a milestone grant is what a founder who can raise elsewhere turns down. Move that accountability into the deal: clean equity on an uncapped SAFE, with the local-impact commitments in the side letter instead. The fund never leads; a quasi-public agency setting the price is a negative signal.

Instrument

Uncapped SAFE

MFN on larger checks. Clean and out of the lead's way.

Accountability

Milestones become terms

The discipline of milestones moves into the SAFE and side letter, not a grant a fundable founder would refuse.

Check size

Big enough to matter

Meaningful in a pre-seed or seed round, never the largest line.

Posture

Value beyond the check

Pilots, diligence bench, regulatory introductions.
